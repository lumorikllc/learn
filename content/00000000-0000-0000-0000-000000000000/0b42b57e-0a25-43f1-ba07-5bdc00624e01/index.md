## Key Concepts & Definitions

**Note Triage**  
The process of reviewing and prioritizing notes to maximize study efficiency.

**Exam Domain**  
A major category in the Network+ objectives (e.g., Networking Concepts).

**Gap Analysis**  
Assessing what material is missing or underrepresented in your notes.

**Categorization**  
Sorting notes into groups based on shared characteristics (here, exam domains).

**Study Roadmap**  
A scheduled plan that maps topics to study sessions over time.

**Metadata Tagging**  
Adding labels or keywords to notes for easy filtering and retrieval.

These concepts interrelate as follows: you first perform **note triage** by scanning your material, use **metadata tagging** and **categorization** to map content to each **exam domain**, and then conduct a **gap analysis**. Finally, you assemble a **study roadmap** that aligns study sessions with domain priorities.

### 3.2 Identifying and Tagging Gaps  
1. Review official objectives for each domain.  
2. Checklist-style, mark each objective as “covered” or “missing.”  
3. Tag missing topics with a “GAP” label.

*Analogy*: Like checking off ingredients before baking—if sugar is missing, you know what to buy.

### 3.3 Organizing Notes into a Structured Outline

## Common Misconceptions & Pitfalls

1. **“My notes already cover everything.”**  
   Corrective: Official objectives often change—always cross-check with the current exam blueprint.
2. **“More notes equal better preparation.”**  
   Corrective: Quality and relevance matter more than quantity. Focus on filling gaps, not bulk.
3. **“Domains don’t overlap.”**  
   Corrective: Many topics intersect (e.g., VLANs in Infrastructure and Security). Use cross-references.
4. **“I can memorize on test day.”**  
   Corrective: Deep understanding comes from organizing and reviewing notes over time, not last-minute cramming.
5. **“Digital tools make categorization automatic.”**  
   Corrective: Automation helps, but you must verify tags manually to avoid misfiling.

## Worked Example Problem

**Problem:**  
You have 80 pages of notes. You want to triage and allocate pages to each domain based on weight. Current counts:  
- Domain 1: 20 pages  
- Domain 2: 30 pages  
- Domain 3: 10 pages  
- Domain 4: 5 pages  
- Domain 5: 15 pages  

1. Calculate ideal page count per domain:  
   - Domain 2 (25%): $$0.25 \times 80 = 20\text{ pages}$$  
2. Identify which domains are over- or under-represented.  
3. Propose how many pages to add or condense for each domain.

**Solution Steps:**  
1. Compute ideal counts:  
   - D1: $$0.20\times80=16$$; D2: 20; D3: $$0.16\times80=12.8\approx13$$; D4: $$0.19\times80=15.2\approx15$$; D5: $$0.20\times80=16$$  
2. Compare with current:  
   - D1: 20 vs 16 → remove or condense 4 pages  
   - D2: 30 vs 20 → condense 10 pages  
   - D3: 10 vs 13 → add 3 pages  
   - D4: 5 vs 15 → add 10 pages  
   - D5: 15 vs 16 → add 1 page  
3. Action plan: draft 13 pages of content for D3–D5, condense 14 pages from D1–D2.

*Commentary:* Each calculation aligns study material with exam emphasis, preventing wasted effort on over-represented domains.
